Iris Classification

January 2nd, 2022

This winter break I decided to try my hand at self-studying ML and TensorFlow. I followed Google's Machine Learning Crash Course and Chollet's Deep Learning with Python, and gained an understanding of some machine learning basics. As an exercise, I trained a multiclass categorization model for the Iris dataset (Jupyter Notebook).

This was a first glance at ML, and I understood the high level process of training a model. Some data science basics (in the form of test/validation sets and general working with data) were introduced as well. However, these felt oriented towards developers whose ultimate goal is implementing ML models as soon as possible. I think it would be nice to gain a solid foundation in the underlying statistics before trying other ML projects, so I look forward to STA314 and STA414.
