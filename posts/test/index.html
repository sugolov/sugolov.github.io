While you're here, let me ask you, have you ever been suspicious of PCA in high dimensions ? Check out the neat results of PCA of high dimensional random walks with comparison to neural network training (2018) by J. Antognini and J. Sohl-Dickstein.

The authors show that the \(k\)-th principal component of a random walk corresponds to (asymptotically, in dimension) the \(k\)-th Fourier mode.
